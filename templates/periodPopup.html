Would you like some advice to help you through shark week?

  • What should I eat?
  • What should I do?
  • I'm in a bad mood.

Things you SHOULDN'T consume

  • Dairy
  • Sugary Foods
  • Salty Foods
  • Caffeine
  • Fatty Red Meats
  • Cold Foods

Things you SHOULD consume

  • Water

    • Prevents acne, keeps away cramps, helps with bloating.

  • Calcium

    • Helps with cramps.

  • Dark chocolate

    • Satisfies cravings, relaxes muscles, happier. Less fat and does not contain dairy like other chocolates.

  • Celery

    • Celery is filled with water and has close to no calories.

  • Hummus

    • A great source of protein, the chickpeas used to make it helps with sleep and improves your mood.

  • Pineapple

    • Pineapple helps your muscles relax, boosts your mood, and relieves bloating.

  • Non-Caffeinated Tea

    • Green tea helps with cramps. White peppermint tea soothes an upset stomach.

  • Leafy Green Vegetables

    • Leafy green vegetables such as spinach are high in vitamins and minerals. Those high in calcium can greatly help with cramps.

  • Salmon

    • Packed full with healthy fats, eating salmon prior to getting your period can help reduce the symptoms of PMS such as cramping and bloating.

Things you SHOULD do

  • Exercise

    • The best form of exercise for relieving menstrual pain is aerobic exercise. Exercise in general releases happy hormones into the body that make you happier and counters the cramp causing chemicals released during your period. Getting your blood pumping through your body helps relieve cramps.

  • Warm Up

    • Taking a hot shower or bath can help relax your muscles. Heat helps open blood vessels and improves blood flow through your body to relieve menstrual cramps.

  • Rest

    • If possible, be sure to get some rest. Bleeding from your uterus is exhausting.

Hope you feel better! :)
